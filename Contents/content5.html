Squid Game – A Fight for Survival (Show Review)

28 Jan 2025 ⁃ Ravi Prasad Kalauni

  • Show name: Squid Game (2021)
  • Director: Hwang Dong-hyuk
  • Language: Korean (Hindi dubbed)
  • Genre: Thriller, Drama
  • Starring: Lee Jung-jae, Park Hae-soo, Wi Ha-joon, and Jung Ho-yeon
  • Budget: Unknown
  • Box office: N/A (Streaming on Netflix)

I watched Squid Game in 2025, even though it came out in 2021. I had heard so much about it, people saying it’s a must-watch, but never got around to it until recently. And let me tell you, I was hooked from the start. This series is a real rollercoaster of emotions – you go from feeling scared to sad to angry all in one episode. It's a show that really makes you think about life, money, and what people are capable of doing for survival.

Plot of the Show: Desperate for Money

The story starts with a group of people, all struggling with money, being invited to play children's games for a huge cash prize. The twist? If you lose, you die. It’s all about surviving the games to win, but it’s not easy. The main character, Seong Gi-hun (played by Lee Jung-jae), is a gambling addict with huge debts, a failing marriage, and no real purpose in life. He is one of the 456 players who get selected to take part in this deadly competition.

At first, the players think it’s a joke or a game they can just walk away from. But things take a dark turn when the first game, Red Light, Green Light, ends in bloodshed. Suddenly, everyone realizes that the stakes are real, and they have no choice but to keep playing.

The Games: A Battle of Life and Death

The games are simple, but the consequences are deadly. From Red Light, Green Light to Tug of War and Marbles, every game is more intense than the last. Players form alliances, betray each other, and fight to survive. Gi-hun, along with some other characters, like Cho Sang-woo (Park Hae-soo) and Kang Sae-byeok (Jung Ho-yeon), navigate these deadly challenges while trying to keep their humanity intact.

What really makes the show stand out is how it explores human nature. It shows how, when pushed to the edge, people can become selfish, ruthless, and even inhumane. The show doesn’t just focus on the games but also dives into the backstories of the characters, making you feel for them, even when they make terrible decisions.

The Twist: Secrets and Shocking Revelations

The twist in Squid Game is what makes it even more mind-blowing. As the show progresses, the truth behind the games is slowly revealed. It’s not just a game for money; there’s a bigger, darker purpose. The final episodes will leave you questioning everything you thought you knew about the series.

Visuals and Music

The visual style of the show is very unique. The sets, especially the giant doll in Red Light, Green Light and the games' arenas, are bright and colorful, but the dark undertones make everything feel off. The contrast of colors is unsettling, making you feel anxious even during the most innocent scenes.

The music is another highlight. The soundtrack, composed by Jung Jae-il, really sets the tone. The haunting tunes, especially during the intense game scenes, make your heart race. The music adds to the tension and drama, making everything feel more real and personal.

Conclusion

Squid Game is not just another survival show. It’s a deep dive into the darkness of human nature, greed, and the lengths people will go to for survival. The series shows how the world can push someone to the edge and make them lose everything. It’s emotional, intense, and full of twists that’ll leave you on the edge of your seat.

I highly recommend Squid Game. It’s one of those shows that makes you think about life and what it really means to survive. After watching it, you’ll never look at a simple children's game the same way again.
